Brett Rojas

Biography

Brett Rojas is a student at Indiana University Jacobs School of Music studying Percussion Performance. He has studied classical, non-western, and jazz percussion with professors John Tafoya, Kevin Bobo, Michael Spiro and Steve Houghton. Brett Rojas began his percussion education in the Los Angeles are from middle school through high school. He performed in multiple school ensembles and attended the Idyllwild Arts Summer Program his graduating year of high school. Brett began his studies at Jacobs School of Music in 2014. Since college, Brett has performed and taught in a number of settings. Performances include the Nutcracker Ballet, Brazilian Samba Batucada in Chicago, a Puerto Rico benefit concert with La Orquesta de Esquela Vieja, and more. His teaching includes high school drumlines in Los Angeles and Indianapolis areas, Idyllwild Arts summer music programs, and adult drum education.
